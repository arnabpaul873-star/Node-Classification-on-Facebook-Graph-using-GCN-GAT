Conclusion
◈ GAT — Why It Wins
Attention mechanism learns which neighbours matter most, giving the model the ability to focus on high-quality connections in the noisy Facebook graph. This is especially powerful for minority classes (tvshow) where discriminative edges are sparse. The result: consistently higher accuracy, precision, recall, and AUC across almost every class.
⬡ GCN — Strengths & Limits
Simple, efficient, and well-understood. GCN performs respectably at 73.3% and achieves near-identical AUC for government and politician classes. However, uniform neighbourhood aggregation means it cannot adapt to noisy or irrelevant connections — a fundamental bottleneck in heterogeneous social graphs.
✓ Final Verdict
GAT is the superior model for node classification on the Facebook Page–Page graph, achieving 80% accuracy and 0.92 ROC-AUC, outperforming GCN across nearly all metrics. The attention mechanism proves essential for handling the heterogeneous structure and class imbalance of real-world social networks.
